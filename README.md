I'm a PhD researcher in **Electrical Engineering at the University of Rhode Island** (CYPHER Lab), working at the intersection of **electrical engineering, computer networking, and cybersecurity**. I program security logic directly into the network switches that carry power-grid and ICS/SCADA traffic — obfuscating devices against reconnaissance, deploying data-plane decoys, and catching threats at wire speed.

Before research, I spent **eight years running IT/OT and SCADA systems at Ghana Water Company**, a national water utility — so I build systems meant to survive production, not just a paper. My work is jointly funded by the **Office of Naval Research (ONR)** and the **National Science Foundation (NSF)**.

## 🔬 Research Focus

- **Programmable Network Defense** — P4/SDN security architectures for real-time detection and autonomous mitigation at the data plane (BMv2, Intel Tofino ASICs)
- **ICS/SCADA Obfuscation & Deception** — anti-reconnaissance and data-plane decoys (e.g. DNP3 outstations) that mislead attackers while gathering intelligence
- **ML-Driven Threat Detection** — GRU/RNN models for traffic anomaly detection and lightweight classifiers for resource-constrained ICS/OT edge devices
- **Risk Quantification & Governance** — FAIR quantitative risk modeling, FDNA cascading-failure analysis for power grids, and multi-jurisdictional AI governance

## 📌 Featured Projects

- **[Agent-P4-SDN-Based DNS Threat Defense](https://github.com/akekulip/Agent-P4-SDN-Based-DNS-Threat-Defense)** — Closed-loop autonomous defense pipeline that detects and mitigates DNS-based attacks using P4 programmable switches and SDN orchestration.
- **[dnp3_decoy](https://github.com/akekulip/dnp3_decoy)** — Programmable data-plane decoy that impersonates DNP3 outstations (RTUs) on a Tofino switch, presenting attackers with realistic virtual devices, MAC addresses, and OS fingerprints.
- **[adaptive_routing](https://github.com/akekulip/adaptive_routing)** — P4-based adaptive load balancer that distributes traffic across equal-cost paths using real-time link telemetry, improving on static ECMP — applicable to resilient grid communications.
- **[Quantifying Systemic Risk in Critical Power Infrastructure (FDNA)](https://github.com/akekulip/Quantifying-Systemic-Risk-in-Critical-Power-Infrastructure-Using-FDNA)** — Functional Dependency Network Analysis modeling single-node failures into grid-wide cascades to prioritize resilience investment.
- **[MemProof](https://github.com/akekulip/MemProof)** — Admission-control protocol defending against external corpus poisoning in RAG systems by unauthorized sources.
